Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

This policy explains what Receipt Vault collects, why we hold it, where it travels, and the honest limits of the service. Receipt Vault is a free browser app for photographing receipts and keeping a running record of what you spend. Please read the section on risks and limitations. It matters here more than usual.

Who we are

Receipt Vault is a personal portfolio project. The data controller responsible for your personal data is:

Receipt Vault (demo project)

Email: demo@receiptvault.example

If you have any question about how your data is handled, write to the address above. We answer to that email.

What we collect

We collect only what the app needs to work. There is no separate sign-up form, no marketing list, and no payment data. The app is free and takes no card details.

Account details from Google

You sign in with your Google account through Firebase Authentication. When you do, Google passes us three things about your account: your display name, your email address, and your profile photo URL. Firebase also assigns your account a unique identifier (a user ID) that we use to keep your receipts separate from everyone else's. We do not receive your Google password.

Receipt records

For each receipt you save, we store a record containing: the store name, a category, the amount, the date, a link to the receipt image, your account's user ID, and a timestamp of when the record was created.

Receipt images

The photo you take or upload is compressed in your browser to a JPEG (scaled to a maximum width of 1200 pixels, at reduced quality) and then uploaded to an image hosting service. The link to that image is what we store in your receipt record.

What we do not collect

We do not ask for your address, phone number, or payment details. We do not build a profile of you for advertising. We do not sell data to anyone.

How we use it

  • To sign you in and keep you signed in across visits.
  • To show you, and only you, the receipts saved to your account.
  • To read the store, amount, and date from a receipt photo so you do not have to type them (see the note on on-device text recognition below).
  • To calculate your monthly and yearly totals and draw the charts on your dashboard.
  • To let you export your own records as a CSV file or a JSON backup.

We do not use your receipts or account details for any purpose beyond running the app for you.

Text recognition runs on your device

When the app reads the store, amount, and date off a receipt, that text recognition happens inside your own browser. The image is not sent to a server for reading. It is processed locally on your device, and only the fields you keep (plus the compressed image, once you save) leave your device. Text recognition is imperfect and you can edit every field before saving.

Legal bases for processing

If you are in the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area, the General Data Protection Regulation requires us to have a legal basis for using your data. Ours are:

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)). We process your account details and receipt records because you have asked us to provide the app, and we cannot do that without them.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)). We rely on your consent for any non-essential cookies or analytics. You give that consent through the cookie banner, and you can withhold it by choosing essentials only. You can withdraw it at any time.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)). We rely on this narrow basis to keep the service secure and working, for example enforcing the rule that you can only read your own records.

Third parties and where your data goes

Receipt Vault does not run its own servers for accounts or storage. It relies on a small set of third-party services, each of which processes some of your data on our behalf:

  • Google Firebase (Authentication and Firestore). Handles your sign-in and stores your receipt records. Your account details and receipt data live in Google's Firestore database. See Firebase's privacy information and Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Cloudinary. Hosts your compressed receipt images. The stored image is reachable at a web link that we keep in your receipt record. See Cloudinary's Privacy Policy.
  • GitHub Pages. Serves the app's static files (the pages and scripts) to your browser. Like any web host, it may log basic request information such as IP addresses. See the GitHub Privacy Statement.

The app also loads a few software libraries from public content delivery networks so it can run in your browser. These providers may see your IP address as a normal part of delivering those files.

Cookies and local storage

Firebase Authentication sets cookies and uses your browser's local storage to keep you signed in between visits. These are essential. Without them you would have to sign in again on every page load, so they cannot be turned off while you use the app.

When you first visit, a cookie banner asks whether you also allow non-essential cookies. Your choice is saved in your browser's local storage under the key rv_consent so we only ask once. No third-party analytics or advertising cookies load unless you choose Accept all.

Our Firebase configuration includes a Google Analytics measurement identifier. If you accept all cookies, Google Analytics may be used to collect basic, aggregated usage information. If you choose essentials only, it is not used. There are no advertising cookies either way.

Data retention

We keep your account details and receipt records for as long as you use the app. When you delete a receipt, the app removes its record from the Firestore database, and it disappears from your account straight away.

You should know one thing plainly. Deleting a receipt removes the database record, but the receipt image on Cloudinary is not deleted. The image was uploaded using an unsigned upload preset, and images uploaded that way cannot be deleted from within the browser without exposing a secret credential in public code. So the image itself stays on Cloudinary at its existing web link even after you delete the receipt. If you want an image removed from Cloudinary, contact us at the email above and we will remove it manually.

If you want your whole account and all its records erased, write to us and we will delete them.

International transfers

The services above are operated by companies based in the United States and run on infrastructure spread across several countries. That means your data may be stored and processed on servers outside your own country, including in the United States. Where the law requires it, these providers rely on their own safeguards for such transfers, such as standard contractual clauses. You can read the details in the providers' own privacy policies linked above.

Security

Sign-in is handled by Google, so we never see or store your password. Access to your receipts is restricted by database security rules keyed to your account's user ID, which means one account cannot read another account's records. Traffic between your browser and the services travels over encrypted connections (HTTPS).

Risks and limitations

We would rather be straight with you than promise more than a small project can deliver. Please weigh these points before you store anything sensitive here.

  • No system is perfectly secure. Reasonable measures reduce risk. They do not remove it. We cannot guarantee that data will never be accessed by someone who should not have it.
  • Receipt images are not access controlled. Because images are uploaded with an unsigned preset, each one sits at a public web link on Cloudinary. The link is long and hard to guess, but it is not protected by a login. Anyone who obtains the link can view that image. Treat receipt photos as if they could become visible, and avoid uploading receipts that show highly sensitive information.
  • Deleted images persist. As explained under data retention, deleting a receipt does not remove its image from Cloudinary automatically.
  • Text recognition is imperfect. The store, amount, and date read from a photo can be wrong, so check them. This affects accuracy, not privacy, but it is worth knowing.
  • This is a portfolio project. It is offered as-is, maintained on free service tiers, and it is not a commercial product with guaranteed uptime, backups, or support. Keep your own copies of anything important, which the export feature makes easy.

Your rights

Depending on where you live, you have rights over your personal data. These include:

  • Access. Ask what data we hold about you.
  • Correction. Ask us to fix data that is wrong. You can also edit most receipt fields yourself in the app.
  • Deletion. Delete individual receipts in the app, or ask us to erase your whole account. Please note the Cloudinary image caveat above.
  • Portability. Export your own records at any time from the Export tab, as a CSV file or a JSON backup, and take them elsewhere.
  • Withdraw consent. Change your cookie choice or ask us to stop any consent-based processing.

To exercise any of these, write to us at the contact email. If you are in the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area and you think we have mishandled your data, you also have the right to complain to your local data protection authority (a supervisory authority). We would appreciate the chance to sort it out first.

Children

Receipt Vault is not intended for children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect data from them. If you believe a child has used the app and given us their data, contact us and we will delete it.
