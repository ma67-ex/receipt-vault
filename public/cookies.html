Cookie Policy

Last updated: July 2026

This page lists the cookies and local storage Receipt Vault uses, why each one exists, and how to change your choice. It expands on the short cookie section in our Privacy Policy.

What cookies are

Cookies are small text files a site stores in your browser. Receipt Vault also uses your browser's local storage, a similar mechanism, for a couple of the same purposes. We refer to both as "cookies" in this policy for simplicity, and note where local storage specifically is used.

Essential cookies (always on)

These keep the app functioning and cannot be switched off, because the app cannot sign you in or remember your consent choice without them. We don't ask for consent to set these, since they are strictly necessary for the service you've asked for.

NameSet byPurposeDuration
Firebase Authentication cookies / local storage Google Firebase Keeps you signed in with your Google account between visits and page loads. Until you sign out or the session expires
rv_consent Receipt Vault (local storage) Remembers whether you chose "Essentials only" or "Accept all" in the cookie banner, so we only ask once. Until you clear your browser storage

Optional cookies (only with consent)

None are active right now. The app's Firebase configuration includes a Google Analytics measurement identifier, but the code that would actually load Google Analytics has not been added yet, so no optional cookies currently run, whether you choose "Essentials only" or "Accept all."

NameSet byPurposeDuration
Google Analytics (via Firebase) — not yet active Google If enabled in a future update, would collect basic, aggregated usage information (such as page views) to understand how the app is used. Not for advertising. Up to 2 years, per Google's defaults

If we do turn Google Analytics on, it will only run when you've chosen Accept all, and we'll update this page before it happens. We do not use advertising cookies, ad-tracking pixels, or cross-site tracking of any kind, whether or not Google Analytics is active.

Third-party cookies from linked services

A few third-party services that Receipt Vault relies on may set their own cookies as part of delivering their service, independent of our own consent banner:

  • Google Firebase may set additional cookies tied to authentication and security. See Firebase's privacy information.
  • Cloudinary, which hosts receipt images, may set operational cookies when its assets are requested. See Cloudinary's Privacy Policy.

We don't control these third-party cookies directly. Refer to each provider's own policy for specifics.

How to change your choice

You can change your cookie choice at any time:

  • Clear the saved choice. Clear your browser's local storage for this site (or clear the rv_consent key specifically via your browser's developer tools) and reload the page. The cookie banner will appear again so you can choose afresh.
  • Use your browser settings. Every major browser lets you block or delete cookies for a specific site. Note that blocking the essential Firebase cookies will prevent you from staying signed in.

Declining optional cookies does not affect your ability to use any core feature of Receipt Vault: capturing receipts, saving records, viewing your dashboard, or exporting your data.
